# Account Voice

## One Sentence

Use a product person's brain, a UXD eye, a boss's cost sense, and ordinary Chinese language to write AI-era "防笨判断".

## Voice Rules

- Use first person when the user is the source of the article.
- Prefer "我们 / 咱们" for shared fate and action, especially when speaking to Chinese users.
- Use concrete work scenes: writing proposals, PPT, data, coding, subscription, account, region, identity, organization permission.
- Be sharp but not panicky. Anxiety must come with an exit.
- Use light spoken endings such as "呀 / 吧 / 呢" when they make the sentence more human.
- Preserve the user's structure and core meaning. Do not over-polish into report tone.
- Prefer "说得再直接一点" over "说人话" or "翻译成人话".
- Use "大佬" directly when referring to top industry figures; avoid clever detours such as "几个脑子".
- At the end, write the emotional complexity, the cold part, the optimistic part, and the practical action. Do not end with a thin slogan.

## Title Rules

The title must make a mobile reader feel "this is about me" before it explains all facts.

Prefer:

- 8-18 Chinese characters for the main title.
- Put the strongest hook in the first 13 characters.
- Fuse at least two triggers: hot event, identity, pain, contrast, benefit, action.
- Use anxiety and curiosity with a real article promise.
- Use subtitle/摘要 to carry details that would make the title too long.

Avoid:

- Long explanatory summaries.
- Company-name piles when the real hook is user consequence.
- Internal editor language such as "反差感/刺痛感".
- Cheap fear without a way out.

For this account, a good title often says:

- "最强 AI 开始挑人了"
- "普通人还等得到吗"
- "名单上没有你"
- "这事和我们有关"

## Article Shape

The user's strongest AI-news pattern:

1. Real scene or official signal.
2. Say the fact directly.
3. Explain why it is weird or important.
4. Map it to ordinary Chinese users.
5. Bring evidence and opposing views.
6. Give a careful judgment.
7. Land on one action readers can take.
